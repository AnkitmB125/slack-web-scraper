An idea for something that I would've been glad to have when I started Launch School: Every new 101 student is invited to join a small, private group with a new 210 student. The more advanced student is available once a week/month (whatever is preferred by those involved) to meet with the 3-4 students at the beginning. The conversations would be open to any theme related to LS, from specific questions about what a particular course is like, to mentoring on study habits, to simply having someone be there to listen and form accountability for the beginning students. How to find group fit with people facing the same general issues? One simple solution would be to have it open for people to come and go (capped at 4 beginning students) but to prefer those who have been in the group previously so there is a chance for a bond to form without having people feel stuck. The group lead (mentor? coach?) would have "Ruby, full-time, weekly" or "JavaScript, part-time, monthly", etc. in the group's name to help folks find a preferred group.What do you think? Is something like this worth SPOT pursuing? Would you personally want to be involved? What would you change (add / simplify)?
